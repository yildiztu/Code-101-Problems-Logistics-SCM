# 55. The Newsvendor Problem

## 📋 Problem Overview

Marina Rodriguez manages procurement for **Seasonal Sportswear Solutions**, a company specializing in high-end winter sports equipment. Every September, she faces a critical decision that will determine the company's profitability for the upcoming ski season: how many units of their premium ski jackets should she order from manufacturers in Southeast Asia?

## 🎯 Solution Approaches

### **Tier 1**: The Mathematical Formulation (Stochastic Programming) — [`P55-Tier-1_executed.ipynb`](./P55-Tier-1_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Single-period decision with uncertain demand
  - Known demand probability distribution


### **Tier 2**: Priority-Based Marginal Analysis Heuristic — [`P55-Tier-2_executed.ipynb`](./P55-Tier-2_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Discrete demand scenarios with known probabilities
  - Marginal analysis approach for decision making


### **Tier 3**: Ant Colony Optimization for Multi-Period Extension — [`P55-Tier-3_executed.ipynb`](./P55-Tier-3_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Multi-period planning horizon with seasonal demand
  - Inventory carryover between periods


### **Tier 4**: Unsupervised Learning for Demand Pattern Discovery — [`P55-Tier-4_executed.ipynb`](./P55-Tier-4_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Historical demand data with hidden patterns
  - Multiple demand clusters or segments


### **Tier 10**: Proactive Demand & Market Shaping — [`P55-Tier-10_executed.ipynb`](./P55-Tier-10_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Company can influence demand through strategic actions
  - Dynamic pricing affects customer purchasing behavior
