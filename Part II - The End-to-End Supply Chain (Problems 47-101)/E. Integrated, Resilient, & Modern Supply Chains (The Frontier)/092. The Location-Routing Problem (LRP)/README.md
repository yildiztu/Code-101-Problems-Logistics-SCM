# 92. The Location-Routing Problem

## 📋 Problem Overview

Imagine a national retail company planning to expand into a new region. The company has identified several potential locations for new distribution centers (DCs) to serve hundreds of retail stores in the area. Each potential DC site has an associated fixed cost for construction and operation. Each retail store has a known demand for products. The company owns a fleet of delivery trucks with limited capacity.

## 🎯 Solution Approaches

### **Tier 1**: The Pen & Paper Method (Mathematical Formulation) — [`P92-Tier-1_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-1_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Fixed costs for opening depots are known
  - Customer demands are deterministic


### **Tier 2**: The Classic Heuristic (GRASP) — [`P92-Tier-2_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-2_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - GRASP uses randomized greedy construction for solution diversity
  - Local search improves solutions through iterative refinement


### **Tier 3**: The Advanced Algorithm (Grey Wolf Optimizer) — [`P92-Tier-3_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-3_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Grey Wolf Optimizer mimics wolf pack social hierarchy and hunting behavior
  - Alpha, Beta, Delta wolves guide the search (best solutions)


### **Tier 4**: The AI/ML/RL Augmentation Method (Transformers) — [`P92-Tier-4_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-4_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Transformer architecture uses self-attention for spatial relationship learning
  - Customer embeddings capture location, demand, and routing features


### **Tier 5**: The Integrated Digital Twin — [`P92-Tier-5_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-5_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Digital twin provides real-time synchronization with physical supply chain
  - IoT sensors continuously monitor depot operations and vehicle movements


### **Tier 6**: The Autonomous & Self-Optimizing Ecosystem (Multi-Agent System) — [`P92-Tier-6_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-6_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Multi-agent system with decentralized intelligence and decision-making
  - Customer agents broadcast demand and service requirements


### **Tier 8**: The Value-Aligned & Ethical Framework — [`P92-Tier-8_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-8_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Ethical optimization incorporates societal and stakeholder values beyond pure cost minimization
  - Constitutional AI framework ensures value-aligned decision making


### **Tier 9**: The Quantum Leap (Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm - QAOA) — [`P92-Tier-9_executed.ipynb`](./P92-Tier-9_executed.ipynb)
- **Learning Goals**:
  - Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA) uses quantum circuits for combinatorial optimization
  - QUBO (Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization) formulation converts LRP to quantum-friendly format
